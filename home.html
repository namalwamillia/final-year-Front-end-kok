John's Tomato Revolution

Started: January 2023

John Mukasa had been growing tomatoes for 15 years in the eastern region of Uganda. Despite his experience, he struggled with pest infestations and inconsistent yields, making it difficult to support his family of five.

"I was ready to give up farming altogether," John recalls. "The costs were too high and returns too low."

After attending a King of Kings agricultural workshop, John decided to try our organic pest management solutions and high-yield tomato seeds.

The results were remarkable:

  • Tomato yield increased by 300%
  • Pest damage reduced by 80%
  • Production costs decreased by 35%

Today, John supplies tomatoes to major markets in three districts and has hired four employees from his community.

"King of Kings didn't just sell me products, they gave me a sustainable system that transformed my farm and my life," says John.

Maria's Organic Journey

Maria wanted to transition to organic farming but faced numerous challenges. With our support, she now runs a certified organic farm.

Maria's Organic Journey

Started: March 2022

Maria Achieng inherited her family's 3-acre farm in 2020 with a vision to convert it to organic production. However, she quickly encountered challenges with soil health, certification processes, and market access.

"The transition period was the hardest. Yields dropped initially, and I wasn't sure if I could make it work financially," Maria explains.

King of Kings provided Maria with:

  • Organic soil amendments specially formulated for her soil type
  • Training on natural pest management techniques
  • Connection to organic certification advisors
  • Access to premium markets seeking organic produce

Within 18 months, Maria's farm received organic certification, and she now supplies vegetables to high-end restaurants and supermarkets at premium prices.

"My income has increased by 70% compared to conventional farming, and the environmental impact is something I'm extremely proud of," Maria says.

David's Cassava Innovation

David Okello's cassava farm faced disease and low-yield issues that limited his production. After implementing King of Kings' disease-resistant cassava stems and modern farming techniques, his yield increased from 2.1 to 5.9 tons per acre while reducing crop loss by 45%. His harvest time also decreased by 15 days, improving market timing and profitability.

Sarah's Community Garden

Sarah transformed unused land into a thriving community garden using King of Kings' organic techniques. The garden now provides fresh vegetables to 50 local families, creates part-time employment for 8 community members, and serves as a training center for sustainable urban farming in the area.

Peter's Tractor Hiring Success

Peter started with just 5 acres of manually cultivated land before partnering with King of Kings. After adopting our tractor hiring service and modern farming protocols, his land preparation time dropped from 14 days to under 2 days, and crops were planted within the optimal season window. In two years, he expanded to 50 acres and now supplies produce to markets across the region.
